# BRAND-FACING TERMS

## 1. The basics — who owns what

- **Future Finds retains full copyright and ownership** of all content it produces, and of the Maruri Mori character, likeness, voice, and persona.
- Brands **license** finished content for defined uses, durations, and territories. A license is a rental with clear parameters — not a purchase of ownership.
- The Maruri Mori character herself is never for sale, licence-able as a standalone asset, or transferable. Brands license *finished videos featuring her*, not the character.

## 2. Usage tiers

Every deliverable is licensed under one or more of these tiers. Each tier is a separate line item.

| Tier | What it covers | Standard term |
|---|---|---|
| **T1 — Organic** | Brand posts the content on its own social channels (feed, Stories, TikTok, etc.) with no ad spend behind it | **6 months included** in every base creation fee |
| **T2 — Paid Social** | Brand runs the content as paid ads / dark posts / boosted content from the brand's own ad accounts (TikTok, Meta, YouTube, Pinterest) | Licensed in 30-day blocks |
| **T3 — Whitelisting / Spark Ads** | Brand runs ads through or authorised by the @futurefindsmedia account (TikTok Spark Ads, Meta partnership ads) | Licensed in 30-day blocks |
| **T4 — Other digital channels** | Brand website, product pages, email marketing | Licensed per channel, in 30-day blocks |
| **T5 — Buyout (digital perpetual)** | Perpetual, worldwide digital usage across T1–T4 | One-time fee (see rate card) |

Traditional media (TV, print, out-of-home) is not covered by any standard tier and is quoted case-by-case.

## 3. Duration & renewal

- All timed licenses run from the date of **first use** by the brand (or 14 days after delivery, whichever comes first).
- Renewals are available at the same rate as the original license block and can be agreed by email — no new contract needed.
- If a license expires and isn't renewed, the brand must stop running the content in paid placements. Organic posts already published may remain live unless otherwise agreed.

## 4. Editing & derivative works

- Brands may make **platform-necessary modifications**: cropping, aspect-ratio changes, subtitles, adding end cards / CTAs / logos.
- Brands may **not** re-edit content in ways that change what Maruri says, appears to say, or endorses — no re-cutting her words, no AI manipulation of her face or voice, no compositing her into new footage.
- Need a different hook, CTA, or message? That's a variant, not an edit — and variants are fast and inexpensive (see rate card). This is the advantage of working with us: don't stretch a license, just ask for a new cut.

## 5. Territory

- Standard licenses cover the **United Kingdom**.
- Worldwide digital usage: **+50%** on the applicable license fee.

## 6. Exclusivity

- All work is **non-exclusive by default** — Future Finds may work with other brands, including in adjacent categories.
- **Category exclusivity** is available as an add-on (see rate card): Future Finds will not produce content for direct competitors in the brand's defined product category for the agreed window.
- The category is defined narrowly and in writing at booking — a specific product category (e.g., "home fragrance diffusers"), optionally plus up to 5 named competitors. "Lifestyle" or "wellness" as a whole is not a category.
- Exclusivity windows run from the delivery date.

## 7. What we warrant

- All content is produced end-to-end by Future Finds. Maruri Mori is an original, AI-generated character created from an original text description — **no real person's likeness, image, or voice was used as source material**. There is no third-party talent, and therefore no third-party talent claims, image-rights renewals, or usage disputes — ever.
- Music, if any, will be cleared for the licensed use or supplied by the brand.
- The brand is responsible for the accuracy of all product claims supplied in the brief, and for its own regulatory compliance in paid media (including ad disclosure rules and any platform AI-content labelling settings applicable to advertisers).

## 8. Commercial terms

- **Payment:** 50% on booking, 50% on delivery, before final files are released. Retainers: monthly, in advance.
- **Revisions:** Two revision rounds included on every deliverable. Additional rounds are charged per the rate card. Because there are no reshoots, revisions are genuinely fast.
- **Turnaround:** 3–5 business days from approved script (standard). Rush available — see rate card.
- **Kill fee:** If a project is cancelled after script approval, the booking deposit (50%) is retained.

# INTERNAL NOTES — do not send

## Exclusivity policy (DECIDED, v1.0)

**Default position: non-exclusive, always.** Exclusivity is sold, never given away in negotiation — it's the single best margin add-on we have.

**Pricing (line items on rate card):**
- 30-day category exclusivity: **+30%** of total project fee
- 90-day category exclusivity: **+60%** of total project fee
- 6-month category exclusivity: **+100%** of total project fee
- Longer: custom quote only. Never grant perpetual category exclusivity — it permanently amputates a revenue category.

**Rules of application:**
1. Category must be defined in writing before booking is confirmed — product category + up to 5 named competitors, no broader.
2. Before accepting ANY new enquiry, check `Category_Exclusivity_Log.csv` for conflicts.
3. Log every deal AND every organic feature in the log — organic features aren't contractual conflicts, but pitching NEOM's competitor two weeks after featuring NEOM organically is a trust/brand-judgement call you want to make deliberately, not discover by accident.
4. If a brand requests exclusivity mid-negotiation, it is always additive to price — never absorbed as a discount concession.

## Pricing rationale (from 2026 market research)

- Industry standard paid-usage pricing: +20–30% of base per 30 days. Whitelisting: +50–100%. 30-day exclusivity: 1.3–1.5x; 90-day: 1.5–2x; perpetual buyout: 2.5–4x. Our numbers sit inside these bands.
- Organic-included-6-months matches the standard 3–6 month band at the generous end — deliberately, as a differentiator that costs us nothing.
- The "no re-editing, buy a variant instead" clause (§4) is deliberate: it converts a rights-policing problem into a revenue stream, and variants are where AI production margin is strongest.
