**CREATIVE SERVICES AGREEMENT**

Between **[Client legal name]** ("Client") and **[Operator legal name / trading as Future
Finds Media]** ("Studio"), dated **[date]**.

### 1. Services & Deliverables
The Studio will produce short-form video advertising creative featuring its virtual presenter
("Maruri Mori"), per the deliverables schedule below:

| Deliverable | Qty | Format | Fee |
|---|---|---|---|
| [e.g. Hook-Test Bundle: 3 hook variations + 1 body video] | [1] | [9:16 MP4, 15–45s] | [£150] |

Scripts are approved by the Client in writing before production. What is approved is what is
produced; material changes after approval constitute a new deliverable or a paid revision
round (two revision rounds are included per deliverable).

### 2. Turnaround
Standard delivery is **48 hours from written script approval** per batch of up to [3] videos,
unless otherwise agreed in the schedule above.

### 3. Payment
50% of the total fee on signing; 50% on delivery. Retainers are billed monthly in advance.
Invoices are payable within [14] days. Fees are exclusive of VAT [if/where applicable].

### 4. Licence & Usage Rights
On receipt of full payment, the Client receives a **perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive
licence** to use the delivered video files across digital channels, including paid social
(dark posts, boosted content, brand ad accounts), organic social, websites, email, and
landing pages. Traditional media (TV, print, out-of-home) requires a separate licence.

★ **4.1 Finished files only; no likeness or model rights.** The licence covers the finished,
delivered video files only. No rights are granted in the presenter's likeness, face, voice,
character, or any source, intermediate, or raw materials. The Client shall not, and shall not
permit any third party to, use any delivered content or any element of the presenter's
likeness or voice **to train, fine-tune, or prompt any generative AI model**, to create
derivative synthetic performances, or to generate new content featuring the presenter.
Editing delivered files for length, cropping, captions, or platform formatting is permitted.

### 5. AI Disclosure & Advertising Compliance
★ **5.1** The Studio discloses in writing (and by this clause does disclose) that the
presenter is AI-generated. As the party deploying the advertising, the **Client is
responsible for** (a) applying any AI-content labels or disclosures required by the platforms
and applicable law/regulation in the territories where the creative runs, and (b) the
substantiation and legality of all product and efficacy claims in approved scripts. The
Studio warrants the creative will not present the presenter as giving personal testimony of
product use (presenter-framing per ASA/CAP guidance); claim substance is supplied and owned
by the Client.

### 6. Exclusivity
None by default. Category exclusivity is available as a paid add-on per the rate card and, if
purchased, is defined in writing here: [category, territory, duration].

### 7. Portfolio Use
The Studio may display delivered work in its own portfolio and pitch materials after the
campaign's public launch, unless the Client opts out in writing here: [ ].

### 8. Term & Termination
Either party may terminate for material breach unremedied within [14] days of written notice.
Fees for delivered or in-production work remain payable. Clauses 4, 5, and 9 survive.

### 9. General
No employment, agency, or partnership is created. Each party's total liability under this
agreement is capped at the fees paid or payable under it (nothing limits liability for fraud
or where the law does not permit limitation). This agreement is governed by the laws of
**England and Wales**, and the parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of its courts.

## Negotiation guardrails (internal — not part of the contract)
- ★ Clauses 4.1 (no-training/likeness) and 5.1 (disclosure + claims responsibility) are
  never removed or diluted. A brand that pushes hard on 4.1 is trying to clone the presenter
  — walk away (playbook §5 red flag).
- Requests for raw/green-screen/voice files: refuse; offer re-edits of finished files instead.
- "Pay in product": decline — presenter is virtual; nothing to receive (playbook §5).
- Rush (24h) is +40% per the rate card; never promise faster than the pipeline has proven.
