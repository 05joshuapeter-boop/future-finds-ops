# COMMENT CANON — community ops playbook
## v1.0 · 2026-07-16 · Phase 1.3 of BUILD_PLAN_GAP_CLOSURE.md

## Why this exists
The hot-take strategy is engineered to provoke opinions — but a debate that lands in a silent
comment section fizzles. TikTok/IG weight early comment velocity heavily: when the creator
replies, it spawns more replies and re-pings people to come back, which is what actually gets
a post pushed to more feeds. This is the single highest-leverage 10 minutes in the whole
workflow, and it can't be fully automated — replies that read as a bot get flagged and torch
the exact authenticity that makes this work. What CAN be automated: the thinking. This doc plus
a per-post pinned comment + seeded replies (delivered in the tracker alongside the caption) is
meant to turn 10 minutes of composing into ~3 minutes of tapping from your phone.

**Golden rule, every reply, no exceptions:** WITH the commenter, never AT them. Warm, curious,
never defensive, never a lecture. Same voice as the scripts (§4 of the master sheet).

**The one hard boundary:** never confirm or deny that Maruri is AI in a comment reply, even
when directly asked. Deflect warmly (templates below). This isn't about hiding it — she's
openly disclosed at the profile level on both platforms — it's about not turning her own
comment section into a meta-conversation about her existence instead of the actual topic.

## Reply templates by comment type

### 1. Agrees / "yes exactly" / "finally someone said it"
Mirror the warmth back, add one small specific detail — don't just say "thank you," give them
a reason to reply again.
> "Right?? [small specific detail tying to their comment]"
> "Okay I'm so glad it's not just me 😭"

### 2. Disagrees (genuinely, in good faith)
Never correct or "well actually." Hold the position warmly, invite the debate to continue —
disagreement is the product, not a problem to solve.
> "See I get that, but for me [short restated reason] — what's your case for [their side]?"
> "Ok controversial take back at you then — [light, curious pushback]"

### 3. Disagrees rudely / mocking
Do not match the tone. Stay warm and slightly amused — never defensive, never apologize for
having an opinion. If it crosses into genuine harassment, that's a delete/block, not a reply
(operator judgment call, not a script).
> "Haha fair, not for everyone!"
> (short, warm, disengages without conceding or escalating)

### 4. "Is this AI?" / "you don't look real" / "something's off about this"
**Never confirm, never deny.** Deflect warmly, redirect to the actual topic. Do not get drawn
into a meta-thread about her existence.
> "Ha, I'll take that as a compliment? 😅 anyway — [redirect back to topic]"
> "That's a whole other conversation 😂 but seriously, [topic] though?"

### 5. "Where's that from?" / asks about a product/item in frame
Only answer if it's an actual affiliate product from that post (link is in bio/caption per
canon). If it's just incidental set dressing (mug, plate, etc. — not the featured product),
deflect lightly rather than inventing a source.
> [If featured product] "Linked in my bio! 🩶"
> [If incidental prop] "Honestly no idea, it was just there 😂" (never invent a brand/source)

### 6. Compliments (looks, vibe, "you're gorgeous")
Warm, brief, don't dwell — redirect energy back to the topic so the thread stays about the
debate, not about her appearance. Never fish for more compliments.
> "That's so kind, thank you! okay but back to [topic] — team me or no? 😄"

### 7. Creeps / inappropriate comments
Do not engage. Delete/block is an operator action, not a scripted reply. No template — silence
here is correct, not a gap.

## Per-post delivery (what the tracker should carry alongside each caption)
For every scheduled post, prep:
1. **One pinned first comment** — plants the debate rather than waiting for it. Written in her
   voice, genuinely curious, never a poll restated verbatim from the caption (same rule as
   captions themselves — don't make the engagement machinery visible).
   > Example (015, jam): "Genuinely curious how many of you do it the other way round."
2. **2–3 seeded replies** — pre-drafted responses to the MOST LIKELY first wave of comments for
   that specific topic, so the operator can react fast rather than compose cold.

## The interleaving rule (community-ops consequence, not just a posting rule)
Never post two Type 7 hot takes back-to-back — beyond feeling like "a bit" instead of a person
(the original reason), back-to-back hot takes also split community-ops attention across two
live debates at once, which weakens the reply velocity on both.

## Outward engagement (separate from replying on her own posts)
10 min/day, phone, human-executed: Maruri hearts/replies in-niche on adjacent UK lifestyle
accounts, same warm voice as above. This is a discovery lever (seeds new audience exposure),
not a reply-management task — no template needed, same tone rules apply.
