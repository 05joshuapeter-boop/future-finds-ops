# 1. PRIMARY PERSONA: "The Discerning Discoverer"

This is the core audience Maruri is built for. Every content decision should serve this person first.

---

| Field | Value |

| **Gender Split** | ~65% Female / 35% Male |

---

## 1.2 Psychographic Profile

### Identity & Self-Image
- Considers themselves to have *taste* — quality over quantity mindset
- Not a maximalist shopper. Buys less, but buys better.
- Slightly skeptical of influencer culture — but follows the rare ones they trust
- Aspires to a "curated life" — aesthetic, functional, intentional

### Relationship to Technology & Products
- Tech-comfortable, not tech-obsessed
- Interested in products that solve real problems elegantly
- Susceptible to "why didn't I think of that" products
- Researches before purchasing (Amazon reviews, Reddit, YouTube)

### Relationship to Social Media
- TikTok: discovery mode — finds things via FYP, not search
- Instagram: aspirational browsing — follows aesthetic accounts, saves Reels
- Doesn't want to be sold to. Wants to *discover*.
- Responds to personality, not just product

### Buying Behavior
- Impulse buys triggered by: visual appeal + social proof + clear utility
- Hesitation removed by: authentic delivery + specific detail + price accessibility
- Purchase sweet spot: £15–£80 / $20–$100 — low enough for impulse, high enough to feel like an investment
- Amazon Prime users — low friction to purchase

---

## 1.3 Psychographic Triggers (What Makes Them Buy)

| Trigger | How Maruri Activates It |
|---|---|
| **Social Proof** | "I've been using this for 3 months now…" |
| **Specific Detail** | Naming the exact feature that makes something worth it |
| **Discovery Framing** | "I don't know how I didn't know about this" |
| **Aesthetic Alignment** | Product looks good in her space → they want it in theirs |
| **Quiet Authority** | She clearly knows what she's talking about |
| **FOMO (soft)** | "This one's been selling out…" — never manipulative |

---

## 1.4 Content Consumption Behavior

**TikTok**
- Watches: 0–3s hook decides everything
- If hooked: watches 15–30s before deciding to stay or leave
- Shares: Content that makes them look like they have taste / are in-the-know
- Comments: When they relate personally or want to claim discovery ("I have this!!")
- Saves: For products they might actually buy

**Instagram Reels**
- More deliberate than TikTok — follows Maruri more intentionally
- Uses Stories for closer relationship (future priority)
- Profile visits more likely after Reel resonates

---

## 1.5 Why They Follow Maruri Specifically

They don't follow her because she's an AI.  
They follow her because:
- She doesn't feel fake even though she's polished
- She explains *why* things are good, not just *that* they are
- Her taste matches theirs (or who they want to be)
- She feels like a stylish friend who actually researches things
- She's never desperate for their attention

# 3. ANTI-PERSONA (Who We Are NOT Building For)

Identifying who Maruri is *not* for is as important as who she is for.

| Type | Why They're Out of Scope |
|---|---|
| **Deal hunters / couponers** | Price-first mindset conflicts with quality-curation positioning |
| **Trend maximalists** | They want whatever's viral today — Maruri curates, not chases |
| **Older demographics (45+)** | Platform mismatch, aesthetic mismatch |
| **AI-curious viewers** | "She's not real!" novelty wears off fast, doesn't monetize |

# 4. PLATFORM-SPECIFIC BEHAVIOR

## TikTok ICP Behavior
- Discovery via FYP — cold audience
- Attention span: 3s hook required
- If retained past 7s: likely to watch full video
- Saves heavily influence algorithm push
- Product links convert better under £50/$60 price point

## Instagram ICP Behavior
- Warmer audience — more likely to already follow
- Reels: discovery for new followers
- Stories: retention and intimacy with existing followers
- Bio + links: higher-intent traffic
- DMs: quality signal — product questions = near-purchase intent

---

# 5. ICP USAGE IN CONTENT DECISIONS

Before creating any content, run this check:

> **"Would The Discerning Discoverer (22–34, taste-conscious, selective buyer) stop scrolling for this in the first 3 seconds?"**

If no → rework the hook.

> **"After watching, would they feel like they discovered something — not like they were sold something?"**

If no → rework the delivery.

> **"Is the product in their buying sweet spot AND aesthetically credible for their self-image?"**

If no → reconsider the product.
